# Research Question 3: Demographics & Adoption Trends

## Primary Question
Who is adopting AI platforms, and how are demographic patterns evolving across age, gender, geography, and socioeconomic factors?

## Key Hypothesis
AI adoption is following different patterns than previous technology waves, with surprising demographic shifts including gender parity achievement and emerging economy leadership.

## Significance
Understanding demographic patterns is critical for:
- Predicting future adoption curves
- Identifying underserved populations
- Educational policy and curriculum design
- Digital divide mitigation strategies
- Market sizing and opportunity assessment

## Key Findings Summary
- **Gender parity achieved**: ChatGPT now 52.4% female (vs male-dominated in 2024) ([OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/how-people-are-using-chatgpt/))
- **Youth adoption**: 64% of U.S. teens use AI chatbots, 28% daily ([Pew Research](https://www.pewresearch.com/internet/2025/12/09/teens-social-media-and-ai-chatbots-2025/))
- **Age concentration**: 53.24% of ChatGPT users are 18-34 years old ([DemandSage](https://www.demandsage.com/chatgpt-statistics/))
- **Emerging economies leading**: India, Brazil, Mexico, South Africa have highest adoption rates ([Modern Diplomacy](https://moderndiplomacy.eu/2025/03/04/why-is-ai-adoption-slower-in-the-global-south-and-how-can-it-leap-forward/))
- **Racial disparities (U.S. teens)**: Black/Hispanic teens use AI more than white teens ([Pew Research](https://www.pewresearch.com/internet/2025/12/09/teens-social-media-and-ai-chatbots-2025/))
- **Generational divide**: Under-35s far more likely to use and trust AI ([OECD-Cisco](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/oecd-cisco-research-finds-stark-geographical-and-generational-divides-in-ai-uptake-and-digital-well-being-302632418.html))
